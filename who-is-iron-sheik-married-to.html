    The Iron Sheik, born Hossein Khosrow Ali Vaziri on March 15, 1942, is a renowned figure in the world of professional wrestling. Hailing from Iran, he gained international fame during the 1980s for his captivating in-ring skills and his Iranian nationalist character.

    Vaziri began his wrestling career as an amateur, representing Iran in the 1968 Summer Olympics. He transitioned to professional wrestling and found success in promotions like the World Wrestling Federation (WWF, now WWE). The Iron Sheik’s technical prowess and strength made him a formidable opponent, and he captured the WWF World Heavyweight Championship in 1983.

    Known for his signature move, the Camel Clutch, The Iron Sheik became one of the most memorable and controversial figures in wrestling history. His larger-than-life personality, combined with his outspoken nature and colourful interviews, solidified his status as a cultural icon.

    The Iron Sheik’s presence in the wrestling community extends beyond his career. As he remains active through appearances at events and conventions. His lasting impact as a skilled athlete and captivating personality continues to resonate with wrestling fans worldwide.

    About Iron Sheik’s wife

    Hossein Khosrow Ali Vaziri, known as The Iron Sheik, was a Shia Muslim and a former soldier in the Imperial Iranian Army. On March 21, 1976, he married Caryl Peterson, an American woman.

    Their wedding had “Mean” Gene Okerlund, a prominent wrestling personality, serving as the best man. Despite the language barrier, The Iron Sheik often referred to Okerlund as “Gene Mean” in his broken English.

    Tragically, the couple faced a devastating loss when their eldest daughter, Marissa Jeanne Vaziri, was murdered by her boyfriend, Charles Warren Reynolds, in May 2003, at the age of 26. Reynolds was apprehended and later convicted for the crime. In a further sorrowful turn, Reynolds himself passed away on May 31, 2016.

    The Iron Sheik and Caryl Peterson have two other daughters and are grandparents to five grandchildren. The family has undoubtedly faced profound challenges, but their resilience and love for one another have helped them navigate through difficult times.
