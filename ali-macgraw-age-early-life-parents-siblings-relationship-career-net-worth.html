    MacGraw’s rise to stardom began with her role in the 1969 film “Goodbye, Columbus.”

    However, true fame came when she co-starred with Ryan O’Neal in the romantic drama “Love Story” in 1970, which became one of the highest-grossing films in U.S. history.

    This film, particularly MacGraw’s performance, garnered widespread critical acclaim and earned her the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama.

    She also received an Academy Award nomination.

    Following “Love Story,” MacGraw graced the cover of Time magazine.

    In 1972, after just three films, she left her mark with her footprints and autograph at Grauman’s Chinese Theatre.

    Her next significant role was alongside Steve McQueen in “The Getaway” (1972), a box office hit.

    After a five-year acting break, she returned in 1978 with another successful film, “Convoy,” co-starring Kris Kristofferson.

    She continued to appear in movies like “Players” (1979) and “Just Tell Me What You Want” (1980), directed by Sidney Lumet.

    In 1983, MacGraw starred in the highly successful television miniseries “The Winds of War.”

    She also joined the popular ABC prime-time soap opera “Dynasty” in 1985 as Lady Ashley Mitchell, a role she took primarily for financial reasons.

    She appeared in 14 episodes before her character met an unfortunate end in the “Moldavian Massacre” cliffhanger episode.

    Furthermore, MacGraw hosted segments for the Encore Love Stories premium cable network during the late 1990s and 2000s.

    In February 2021, MacGraw and O’Neal received stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, nearly five decades after the release of “Love Story.”

    Ali MacGraw Animal Advocacy

    In July 2006, MacGraw took part in a public service announcement for People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA).

    She emphasized the importance of evacuating pets during wildfires.

    In 2008, she contributed the foreword to the book “Pawprints of Katrina” by author Cathy Scott, which featured photography by Clay Myers.

    The book highlighted the efforts of the Best Friends Animal Society during the largest pet rescue operation in U.S. history.

    MacGraw holds the role of a U.S. Ambassador for the animal welfare charity Animals Asia.

    Her dedication to animal rights advocacy throughout her life earned her the Humane Education Award from Animal Protection of New Mexico for her outspoken support of animal-related issues.

    Ali MacGraw Personal Life

    During her college years, MacGraw met Robert “Robin” Martin Hoen, a Harvard-educated banker of German-Canadian descent, and the two wed on October 29, 1960.

    Their marriage ended in divorce a year and a half later, and Hoen passed away on September 13, 2016.

    Following her first divorce, MacGraw had several relationships and underwent an abortion, which was illegal at the time.

    In 1979, MacGraw’s mother, who was 38 when she had Ali, revealed that she had her own abortion in the early 1920s.

    On October 24, 1969, MacGraw married film producer Robert Evans.

    They have a son, Josh Evans, who is an actor, director, producer, and screenwriter.

    They separated in 1972 after MacGraw became publicly involved with Steve McQueen during the filming of “The Getaway.”

    She married McQueen on July 12, 1973, in Cheyenne, Wyoming, and divorced him in August 1978.

    Following her divorce from McQueen, she had relationships with several individuals, including Warren Beatty, Rick Danko, Bill Hudson, Ronald Meyer, Rod Stryker, Fran Tarkenton, Peter Weller, Henry Wolf, and Mickey Raphael.

    In her autobiography, “Moving Pictures,” MacGraw revealed her struggles with alcohol and sex addiction.

    She received treatment for alcoholism at the Betty Ford Center.

    When her former husband, Robert Evans, was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 2002, MacGraw accompanied him.

    Their grandson, Jackson, was born in December 2010 to Josh and his wife, singer Roxy Saint.

    After Evans passed away in 2019, MacGraw expressed her deep loss and pride in his significant contributions to the film industry.

    Since 1994, MacGraw has resided in Tesuque, New Mexico, following the destruction of her rented house in Malibu due to a fire.
